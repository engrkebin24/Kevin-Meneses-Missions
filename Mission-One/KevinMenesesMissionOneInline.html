Chicken Pork Adobo

Hearty, Tangy, and Savoury

Ingredients

  • 0.5 kg - Chicken Parts
  • 0.5 kg - Pork Belly
  • 1 head - Garlic
  • 0.5 cup - Soy Sauce
  • 6 tbsp - White Vinegar
  • 1 cup - Water
  • 2 tsp - Whole Black Peppercorn
  • 2 pcs - Bay Leaf
  • 1 tbsp - Vegetable Oil

Steps

  1. Combine pork, soy sauce, garlic, vinegar, water, peppercorn and bay leaf in a pot. Cover and simmer for 45 minutes. Strain pork and set aside.

  2. Sauté pork and garlic in oil until light golden brown. Pour cooking liquid and simmer until sauce is thick and coating the pork.

  3. Transfer on a serving plate and serve.

Adobo, celebrated as the Philippines’ culinary gem, encapsulates the nation’s diverse heritage.

This robust stew, often featuring chicken and pork, marinates in a blend of vinegar and soy sauce, a harmonious fusion of Spanish, Chinese, Malay, and indigenous influences.

Spanning 7,641 islands, Filipino cuisine weaves a vibrant tapestry of flavors, with each region adding its unique twist to shared recipes. From Luzon’s zesty adobo to Mindanao’s fiery renditions, every variation of this expansive culinary tradition beckons with irresistible allure—a delicious tribute to the country’s gastronomic richness.

Filipino adobo is hearty, tangy, and savoury. It’s a dish that warms the soul and tantalides the taste buds. Traditionally, it’s served during family gatherings, celebrations, and special occasions. Whether enjoyed with steamed rice or as a filling for sandwiches, adobo is a beloved comfort food that brings people together.
